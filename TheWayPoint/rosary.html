Praying the Rosary

The Rosary is a meditative prayer centered on the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ, contemplated with Mary as a faithful guide. Each decade invites you to pray slowly while holding one mystery before your mind and heart.

Use this page to follow along while someone leads the Rosary. Open the prayer text when needed, choose a set of mysteries, and switch between meditation styles depending on the group: brief mystery focus, Scripture-based prompts, simple reflection, or visualization.

Order of the Rosary

  1. Begin: Make the Sign of the Cross and pray the Apostles' Creed.
  2. Opening prayers: Pray 1 Our Father, 3 Hail Marys for faith, hope, and charity, then 1 Glory Be. The Fatima Prayer may also be added.
  3. First decade: Announce the first mystery and its fruit, then pray 1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, and the Fatima Prayer if desired.
  4. Second decade: Announce the second mystery and pray 1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, and the Fatima Prayer if desired.
  5. Third decade: Announce the third mystery and pray 1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, and the Fatima Prayer if desired.
  6. Fourth decade: Announce the fourth mystery and pray 1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, and the Fatima Prayer if desired.
  7. Fifth decade: Announce the fifth mystery and pray 1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, and the Fatima Prayer if desired.
  8. Conclude: Pray the Hail Holy Queen, the concluding prayer, and the Sign of the Cross.

Full Rosary count: 6 Our Fathers, 53 Hail Marys, 6 Glory Bes, 5 announced mysteries, and commonly 5 Fatima Prayers after the five decades.

Prayer Texts

Sign of the Cross

In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

Apostles' Creed

I believe in God, the Father almighty, Creator of heaven and earth, and in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary, suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, died and was buried; he descended into hell; on the third day he rose again from the dead; he ascended into heaven, and is seated at the right hand of God the Father almighty; from there he will come to judge the living and the dead.

I believe in the Holy Spirit, the holy catholic Church, the communion of saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and life everlasting. Amen.

Our Father

Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name; thy kingdom come; thy will be done on earth as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread; and forgive us our trespasses as we forgive those who trespass against us; and lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Amen.

Hail Mary

Hail Mary, full of grace, the Lord is with thee; blessed art thou among women, and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus. Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners, now and at the hour of our death. Amen.

Glory Be

Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit, as it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.

Fatima Prayer

O my Jesus, forgive us our sins, save us from the fires of hell, lead all souls to heaven, especially those in most need of thy mercy. Amen.

Hail Holy Queen

Hail, holy Queen, Mother of mercy, our life, our sweetness, and our hope. To thee do we cry, poor banished children of Eve; to thee do we send up our sighs, mourning and weeping in this valley of tears. Turn then, most gracious advocate, thine eyes of mercy toward us; and after this our exile, show unto us the blessed fruit of thy womb, Jesus. O clement, O loving, O sweet Virgin Mary.

Pray for us, O holy Mother of God, that we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ.

Concluding Prayer

Let us pray. O God, whose only begotten Son, by his life, death, and resurrection, has purchased for us the rewards of eternal life, grant, we beseech thee, that while meditating on these mysteries of the most holy Rosary of the Blessed Virgin Mary, we may imitate what they contain and obtain what they promise, through the same Christ our Lord. Amen.

Mysteries

Open a set of mysteries below. Within each decade, choose the kind of help you want while praying.

Joyful Mysteries Usually prayed Monday and Saturday

1. The Annunciation

Fruit of the mystery: Humility

Mary receives Gabriel's message with reverence, wonder, and freedom. She does not understand every detail, but she trusts the One who is calling her. Pray this decade asking for a humble heart that can receive God's will without needing to control it.

Luke 1:26-38: Gabriel greets Mary as full of grace and announces that she will bear the Son of God. Mary asks how this can be, listens to the promise of the Holy Spirit, and answers, "Let it be done." Hold that consent in mind with each Hail Mary.

God often begins great work through a quiet invitation. Mary shows that humility is not weakness; it is the courage to stand before God truthfully and say yes. Ask where the Lord is inviting you to trust before every detail is clear.

Picture Mary in stillness as the angel speaks. Notice the room, the silence, the surprise, and the peace that follows surrender. As the decade continues, imagine your own fears becoming quiet enough to hear God's invitation.

2. The Visitation

Fruit of the mystery: Love of neighbor

Mary carries Christ within her and goes in haste to serve Elizabeth. Her joy becomes practical charity. Pray for a love that notices need, moves without delay, and brings Christ's presence into ordinary homes and relationships.

Luke 1:39-56: Mary enters Zechariah's house, Elizabeth is filled with the Holy Spirit, and John leaps in the womb. Mary magnifies the Lord and rejoices in God's mercy. Let this decade become a prayer of praise and service.

Grace is not meant to stay private. Mary does not turn inward after receiving a great gift; she turns toward another person. Consider one person who needs your presence, encouragement, patience, or help.

Walk the hill country with Mary. Feel the effort of the journey and the eagerness of love. See two mothers greeting one another with wonder, gratitude, and holy joy because Christ is already at work among them.

3. The Nativity

Fruit of the mystery: Poverty of spirit

Jesus is born in poverty, silence, and tenderness. The eternal Son enters the world as a vulnerable child. Pray for simplicity, gratitude, and freedom from the false security of possessions, status, or comfort.

Luke 2:1-20; Matthew 1:18-25: Mary gives birth in Bethlehem, lays Jesus in a manger, and shepherds come to adore him. Joseph protects the Holy Family. Let each Hail Mary return your attention to the humility of God made flesh.

The Lord enters the world without display or comfort. He chooses a hidden place and poor witnesses. Ask to recognize him in small duties, unnoticed people, and ordinary moments where love is quietly asked of you.

Stand near the manger. Hear the animals, see the lamp light, and behold the newborn Christ resting in Mary's arms. Let the scene become quiet enough that you can adore rather than analyze.

4. The Presentation

Fruit of the mystery: Obedience

Mary and Joseph bring Jesus to the Temple in faithful obedience to the Law. Their offering is quiet, poor, and complete. Pray for a heart that gives God what is most precious and trusts his providence.

Luke 2:22-38: Simeon receives the child Jesus and calls him a light for revelation. Anna gives thanks and speaks of him to those awaiting redemption. Mary hears that a sword will pierce her soul. Pray with trust and reverence.

Mary offers back to God the Son she has received. True obedience is not mere rule-following; it is love ordered toward God. Offer your responsibilities, family, work, anxieties, and future to the Lord.

Enter the Temple courts. Watch Simeon receive Jesus with aged hands and a heart that has waited faithfully. See Anna's joy and Mary's attentive silence as promise and sacrifice meet in one child.

5. The Finding in the Temple

Fruit of the mystery: Joy in finding Jesus

Mary and Joseph search for Jesus with sorrow and perseverance, then find him in his Father's house. Pray for fidelity when Christ feels hidden and for joy when you rediscover him in prayer, worship, and truth.

Luke 2:41-52: After three days, Jesus is found in the Temple, listening and asking questions among the teachers. Mary treasures these things in her heart. Let this decade deepen your desire to seek Christ faithfully.

There are seasons when Jesus seems absent, even to those who love him deeply. Mary teaches perseverance without bitterness. Keep seeking him through prayer, confession, Mass, Scripture, and faithful obedience.

Feel the anxious search through Jerusalem, the crowded streets, and the ache of not knowing. Then enter the Temple and feel the relief of finding Jesus calm, present, and rooted in the Father's will.

Luminous Mysteries Usually prayed Thursday

1. The Baptism of Jesus

Fruit of the mystery: Openness to the Holy Spirit

Jesus enters the Jordan in humility and is revealed as the beloved Son. The Spirit descends, and the Father's voice speaks. Pray to live from your baptismal identity and to be led by the Holy Spirit.

Matthew 3:13-17; Mark 1:9-11: Jesus is baptized by John, the heavens open, the Spirit descends like a dove, and the Father declares his beloved Son. Let each Hail Mary recall your own call to belong to God.

Before Jesus begins his public mission, he receives the Father's affirmation. Your worth also begins in being loved by God, not in achievement. Ask to hear your life from the Father's voice rather than the world's noise.

Stand on the bank of the Jordan. See the water, the crowd, the open sky, and the Spirit descending upon Christ. Imagine the Father's words settling over Jesus, and let them draw you into trust.

2. The Wedding at Cana

Fruit of the mystery: To Jesus through Mary

Jesus works his first sign through Mary's intercession. She notices the need and directs the servants to obey him. Pray for confidence in Mary's care, prompt obedience to Christ, and trust in his abundance.

John 2:1-12: At Cana, Mary tells Jesus, "They have no wine," then tells the servants to do whatever he says. Water becomes wine, and the disciples begin to believe. Let this decade become a prayer of trustful intercession.

Mary brings human need to Jesus without panic. She does not solve the problem herself; she points to him. Bring Christ what has run dry in your home, vocation, relationships, prayer, or hope.

Imagine the wedding feast, the empty jars, the servants drawing water, and the quiet joy when abundance appears. Watch Mary gently turn attention away from herself and toward her Son.

3. The Proclamation of the Kingdom

Fruit of the mystery: Conversion

Jesus announces the Kingdom and calls every heart to repentance and faith. His mercy is not vague; it invites a new way of living. Pray for conversion, courage, and a deeper love of the Gospel.

Mark 1:14-15; Matthew 5-7: Jesus proclaims that the Kingdom of God is at hand and teaches mercy, purity, forgiveness, prayer, and trust. Let his words examine your heart as you pray each Hail Mary.

The Kingdom is not merely an idea; it asks for a changed life. Name one place where Christ is inviting conversion: a habit, resentment, fear, compromise, or neglected act of love.

Sit among the crowds as Jesus teaches. Hear the authority and tenderness in his voice. Let his words search your motives, heal your fear, and call you forward into a more faithful life.

4. The Transfiguration

Fruit of the mystery: Desire for holiness

Jesus reveals his glory to Peter, James, and John so they can glimpse who he truly is. The Father's command is simple: listen to him. Pray for a desire for holiness rooted in the beauty and majesty of Christ.

Matthew 17:1-8; Luke 9:28-36: Jesus is transfigured on the mountain, Moses and Elijah appear, and the disciples hear the Father's voice. With each Hail Mary, turn again toward Christ as the beloved Son.

God gives glimpses of glory to strengthen disciples for the road ahead. Spiritual consolation is not an escape from the Cross; it prepares us to follow Jesus more faithfully when the mountain moment passes.

Climb the mountain with the disciples. See Christ radiant in light, the apostles overwhelmed, and the cloud overshadowing them. Hear the Father's command to listen, then carry that command into daily life.

5. The Institution of the Eucharist

Fruit of the mystery: Love of the Eucharist

Jesus gives himself as food, sacrifice, and covenant. Before he goes to the Cross, he leaves the Church his Body and Blood. Pray for reverence, hunger for his presence, and gratitude at every Mass.

Luke 22:14-20; John 6:51-58; 1 Corinthians 11:23-26: Jesus takes bread and wine and gives his Body and Blood for the life of the world. Let the decade deepen your faith in his real and abiding gift.

The Eucharist is Christ's self-gift, not a symbol of distance. He remains near, humble, and available. Ask for a heart that approaches Communion with repentance, love, attention, and wonder.

Enter the upper room. Watch Jesus bless, break, and give. Notice the apostles, the table, the nearness of betrayal, and the serenity of Christ offering himself before the Cross.

Sorrowful Mysteries Usually prayed Tuesday and Friday

1. The Agony in the Garden

Fruit of the mystery: Sorrow for sin

Jesus prays in anguish, seeing the weight of sin and the suffering before him, yet he submits to the Father's will. Pray for true contrition, honest repentance, and the grace to remain faithful in anguish.

Matthew 26:36-46; Luke 22:39-46: Jesus prays in Gethsemane while the disciples sleep. He asks that the cup pass, yet surrenders to the Father's will. Let this decade draw you into sorrow for sin and trust in mercy.

Christ enters fear, loneliness, and sorrow without turning away from love. He knows the cost of sin and still chooses redemption. Bring him your dread, guilt, avoidance, and weakness; ask for fidelity rather than escape.

Kneel in the dark garden. Hear the silence, see Jesus bowed in prayer, and notice the sleeping disciples nearby. Stay awake with him in compassion as he chooses the Father's will for love of the world.

2. The Scourging at the Pillar

Fruit of the mystery: Purity

Jesus suffers bodily violence and humiliation for sinners. His innocent body bears the cruelty of sin. Pray for purity of heart, reverence for the dignity of every person, and healing from wounds caused by sin.

John 19:1; Isaiah 53:3-5: Jesus is scourged, and by his wounds we are healed. Let this decade keep you near the suffering Christ, asking him to purify your senses, desires, habits, and relationships.

Sin wounds bodies, relationships, and souls. Christ does not meet our impurity with contempt; he bears suffering to heal and redeem. Ask him to heal what you have harmed and what has harmed you.

Do not look away from Christ's suffering, but do not dwell on violence for its own sake. Stand close enough to grieve, repent, and receive mercy from the One who suffers without hatred.

3. The Crowning with Thorns

Fruit of the mystery: Moral courage

Jesus is mocked as king and crowned with thorns. The soldiers ridicule the very truth they cannot recognize. Pray for humility, courage under contempt, and loyalty to Christ when faithfulness costs reputation or comfort.

Matthew 27:27-31; John 19:2-5: Soldiers clothe Jesus in a robe, place a reed in his hand, press thorns upon his head, and mock him. Behold the man, and ask to recognize the true King beneath shame.

Christ accepts humiliation without hatred. Pride demands victory and admiration; love can endure misunderstanding. Ask for freedom from vanity, revenge, and the need to be approved by everyone.

See Jesus robed in mockery, silent before contempt. Notice the false crown and the true authority beneath it. Place your pride before him and let his humility teach you courage.

4. The Carrying of the Cross

Fruit of the mystery: Patience

Jesus carries the Cross through exhaustion, pain, and public shame. Simon is drawn into helping him, and the women of Jerusalem mourn. Pray for patience, perseverance, and compassion for those carrying hidden burdens.

Luke 23:26-32; John 19:16-17: Jesus carries the Cross, Simon of Cyrene is pressed into service, and Jesus speaks to the mourning women. With each Hail Mary, ask to carry your cross with him, not apart from him.

Discipleship includes burdens carried with Christ. Patience is not passivity; it is love that refuses to abandon the good under pressure. Ask for strength for today's cross and gentleness toward others under theirs.

Walk the road to Calvary. Notice the weight, the dust, the faces in the crowd, Simon stepping near, and Christ's steady love. Imagine placing your own burden beneath his gaze.

5. The Crucifixion

Fruit of the mystery: Perseverance

Jesus dies on the Cross for the salvation of the world. He forgives, entrusts Mary and John to one another, and gives himself completely. Pray for final perseverance and a deeper love of the Crucified Lord.

Luke 23:33-49; John 19:25-30: Jesus is crucified between criminals, forgives his persecutors, gives Mary as mother, and says, "It is finished." Let this decade become an act of trust in the mercy won on Calvary.

At the Cross, love goes to the end. Nothing in you is hidden from Christ, and nothing is beyond the reach of his mercy if brought to him. Place your sins, griefs, fears, and hopes beneath the Cross.

Stand with Mary at Calvary. Hear the wind, the voices, and the silence around Jesus. Look upon Christ with sorrow, gratitude, and trust in the love poured out for you and for the world.

Glorious Mysteries Usually prayed Wednesday and Sunday

1. The Resurrection

Fruit of the mystery: Faith

Jesus rises from the dead in victory. The tomb is empty, fear begins to break, and the disciples are called from grief into witness. Pray for faith in the risen Christ and for new life where hope has grown weak.

Matthew 28:1-10; John 20:1-18: The women find the empty tomb, Mary Magdalene encounters the risen Lord, and the disciples begin to believe. Let this decade anchor your faith in Christ's victory over sin and death.

The Resurrection means sin and death do not have the last word. Christian hope is not optimism; it is trust in a living Lord. Ask Christ to raise what is deadened in your faith, charity, courage, or joy.

Stand near the empty tomb at dawn. Feel the shock, fear, and joy as the news begins to spread. Imagine hearing your own name spoken by the risen Christ, as Mary Magdalene heard hers.

2. The Ascension

Fruit of the mystery: Hope

Jesus ascends to the Father and sends his disciples on mission. He is not absent; he reigns and intercedes. Pray for hope, trust in heaven, and courage to witness where Christ has placed you.

Acts 1:6-11; Luke 24:50-53: Jesus blesses the disciples, ascends, and promises the Spirit's power. The angels tell them not to remain staring upward. Let this decade unite longing for heaven with faithful action on earth.

Christ reigns, yet sends ordinary disciples to witness. Hope lifts your eyes without removing your responsibilities. Ask where you are being sent in your family, work, parish, friendships, or suffering.

Stand with the apostles as Jesus blesses them. Watch him ascend, feel the awe and longing, then lower your eyes to the road of mission ahead. Imagine walking that road with his blessing still upon you.

3. The Descent of the Holy Spirit

Fruit of the mystery: Love of God

The Holy Spirit fills Mary and the apostles at Pentecost. Fearful disciples become witnesses, and the Church begins to proclaim Christ boldly. Pray for zeal, docility, and a deeper love of God.

Acts 2:1-13: The Spirit descends like wind and fire, the disciples speak of God's mighty works, and people hear the message in their own languages. Ask the Spirit to make your life intelligible as a witness to Christ.

The Spirit does not merely comfort; he transforms and sends. Areas of fear can become places of witness. Ask for the gifts needed to speak truth, forgive, serve, and live the Gospel with courage.

Enter the upper room with Mary and the apostles. Hear the rushing wind, see courage replacing fear, and imagine the doors opening. Let the fire of the Spirit move from the room into your own heart.

4. The Assumption of Mary

Fruit of the mystery: Grace of a happy death

Mary is taken body and soul into heaven. In her, the Church sees the promise of resurrection and the destiny of a life wholly given to God. Pray for hope, purity of heart, and the grace of a holy death.

Revelation 12:1; Luke 1:46-55: The Church contemplates Mary's glory in light of God's promise and her Magnificat. Her life magnifies the Lord from earth into heaven. Let this decade renew your hope in eternal life.

Mary's destiny shows what grace intends for humanity: communion with God, body and soul. Heaven is not vague escape; it is fulfillment in Christ. Ask to desire heaven rightly and to live today in that direction.

Imagine Mary received into heavenly joy, not as distant, but as a mother alive in Christ and praying for the Church. Let her assumption make heaven feel more concrete, personal, and near.

5. The Coronation of Mary

Fruit of the mystery: Trust in Mary's intercession

Mary is crowned Queen of Heaven and Earth because she is perfectly united to her Son's victory. Her queenship is maternal service under Christ's reign. Pray for trust in her intercession and loyalty to Christ the King.

Revelation 12:1; Psalm 45:9-17: The Church honors Mary as queen mother in the Kingdom of her Son. Her glory reflects Christ's glory. Let this decade lead you through Mary toward deeper worship of Jesus.

Mary's honor never competes with Christ; it reveals what his grace can do in a human life. Ask her to lead you more completely to Jesus, especially in areas where your trust is weak or divided.

Picture heaven rejoicing as Mary is honored by her Son. See her attention remain maternal and directed toward the Church. Let the scene turn your heart toward worship, confidence, and surrender to Christ.

Leader and Response

When praying in a group, the leader may pray the first half of each prayer and the group may respond with the second half. Move slowly enough that people can breathe, listen, and meditate on the mystery being prayed.

For newcomers, it is enough to follow the rhythm: listen to the mystery, pray the repeated Hail Marys, and let the reflection keep the life of Christ in view.
